Section 09

Key Insights Summary

01

Github & Inbound outperform all channels

Technical platform presence and inbound interest have measurable ROI. These channels convert at 11% and 10.1% respectively vs. 8.2% overall average.

02

Referrals underperform expectations

4.7% hire rate — lowest of all channels, despite being commonly over-trusted. Lowest response rate (59.4%) suggests referred candidates approach the process with less urgency.

03

No response is the #1 pipeline killer

210 of 375 logged rejections. Outreach personalization problem, not a volume problem. 41% of all exits have no reason logged — a data quality gap compounding the issue.

04

Score above 75 is the clearest hiring predictor

Candidates clearing 75 across all three dimensions show 82.6% offer rate. Technical score below 70 = 0% hire rate across 221 candidates. A hard floor, not a soft signal.

05

Stage duration is a positive signal

Among candidates who passed screening, hired candidates stay in process nearly 2× longer than non-hired (32.7 vs 16.9 days). Fast exits predict failure.

06

Recruiter performance is level-dependent

Ana converts 37.5% of Staff candidates. Bruno is consistently strong across all levels. Diego and Fernanda collapse at senior/staff — aligning assignments to seniority strengths could improve conversion without adding headcount.

07

Volume sourcing inflates pipeline without improving conversion

Gustavo: 121 candidates, 5% hire rate. Ana: 75 candidates, 13.3% hire rate. Quality of targeting — not volume — drives conversion.

08

Role-channel combinations matter

Analista de Recrutamento via Github converts at 26.7% — 3× the overall average. Junior hiring is only viable for specific roles; applying it uniformly wastes sourcing effort.

Section 10

Practical Recommendations for Recruiters

1

Prioritize Github and Inbound over mass LinkedIn outreach

Reallocate sourcing effort toward channels that convert, not channels that generate volume.

2

Treat no-response as a quality signal

Personalize outreach referencing specific candidate work. Don't increase volume — increase relevance.

3

Use the 75-point threshold as an early decision signal

Set expectations with hiring managers before investing additional interview cycles in candidates unlikely to clear the bar.

4

Build a warm pipeline from timing and headcount rejects

The 8 candidates who cleared ≥75 across all dimensions but lost to external reasons are the highest-probability future hires. Keep them engaged.

5

Align recruiter assignments to seniority strengths

Route senior and staff roles to Bruno and Ana. Develop Diego and Fernanda's calibration at senior levels through shadowing and scorecard review.

6

Audit the referral process

Add a structured evaluation step before referrals enter the pipeline. Avoid advancing any referral candidate solely because of the channel.

7

Consider repositioning the technical assessment before Interview 1 for high-volume requisitions

Particularly at Junior and Pleno levels. A common concern is that early assessments reduce engagement — this can be mitigated with strong candidate communications that explain the process, set expectations, and commit to timely feedback regardless of outcome. When done well, an early test respects the candidate's time as much as the recruiter's. At Senior and Staff levels, the recruiter conversation should come first — relationship and context matter more, and a cold assessment early risks losing high-value passive candidates.
